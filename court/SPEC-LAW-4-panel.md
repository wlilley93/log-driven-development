# SPEC-LAW-4 (*Reference re Per-Precedent Procedural Rules*): the full Supreme Court panel

The verbatim panel record behind [`SPEC-LAW-4`](SPEC-LAW.md), preserved in full per the
deliver-and-record-in-full discipline (LDD-INV-16). The panel held **5-0 to RULE** (an objector's dismissal
challenge rejected) and was **unanimous on the controlling rule** (the clause-granularity subsumption test); the
disposition for the residual DPR 11.4 split **4-1** (keep-pruned vs the textualist's remove).

Court arc: *In re DPR 11.4* first instance (3-0 KEEP-AS-CITATION-ONLY) → Court of Appeal (3-0 OVERTURN-CROSSREF) →
this Supreme reference (5-0).

---

## The Objector (intervener, DPR 3.3 - the first exercise of the apex-only intervention)

**Recommendation: RULE, do not dismiss.** The dismissal trap is real (the carried-up artifact is one thin residual
rule; DPR 1.2 and the anti-self-litigation warning pull toward dismissal), but the *point of law referred* is not
the residual rule, it is the lawfulness test for an entire **populated, named class**: per-precedent procedural
rules (DPR 6.6, 6.7, 9.4, 11.4, enumerated at DPR 12.3). That class is ungoverned by any invariant rule, is
actively growing under the DPR 12.3 self-amendment engine, and divided the two courts below. Dismissal does not
cure the "litigating its own rulebook ad infinitum" pathology; it perpetuates it, because without a controlling
test every member of the class stays independently re-litigable (the "no precedent to deny against" failure
LDD-INV-17 names). A single apex ruling that fixes the bounded test ends the recurrence for the whole class in one
expenditure - the highest-leverage use of expensive currency, not a waste of it. SPEC-LAW-3 does not settle it
(it governs second *units*, not intra-register procedural-rule proliferation).

---

## Justice Verba Tenenda (textualist - "the words must hold")

**On the objection.** RULE; the objector is right that DPR 11.2 forecloses dismissal (a named, populated class).

**Controlling rule.** *Reference re the Per-Precedent Procedural Rule* (the subsumption test). (a) The category is
lawful in principle, not abolished - DPR self-amendment constitutionally contemplates it; the "always subsumed,
always remove" view would read the fold-in rule out of the code. (b) The marginal-content test (controlling): a
per-precedent rule, or any clause within it, is lawful only insofar as it encodes procedural content the generic
floor (DPR 9.1, 9.2, 11.3) does not already carry; a clause that merely restates a generic rule is struck under
LDD-INV-9.

**Disposition.** REMOVE-11.4, on this record: applying the test clause by clause, every surviving clause is wholly
within the generic floor - "proposer bears the burden, on the DPR 9.1 ground-truth standard" *is* DPR 9.1 cited by
number; "decided as a point of law (DPR 9.2)" *is* DPR 9.2; the default-consolidation clause *is* DPR 11.3. Nothing
non-generic remains once the substantive sentence is owned by SPEC-LAW-3. Reduce to deletion-with-citation.

**Self-dissent (verbatim).** "The strongest argument against my holding is that the safe-harbour smuggles a
substantive design judgment into a rule that claims to be pure law, and that limbs (a) and (b) are in latent
contradiction this case papers over. If DPR 12.3 truly *enacts* the per-precedent form, then a court that prunes
every clause to nothing has, in practice, abolished the category it claims to preserve."

## Justice Telos (purposive - a rule is read by the failure it exists to prevent)

**On the objection.** RULE; the objection is sustained on every operative ground.

**Controlling rule.** *Reference re Per-Precedent Procedural Rules* (the procedural-residue doctrine). A
per-precedent procedural rule is neither always subsumed nor always permissible; it is lawful only at clause
granularity, and only for clauses that add procedural content the generic floor does not already carry. (a) The
granularity limb: a single clause cannot be half-procedural; a clause restating a substantive SPEC-LAW standard is
owned by SPEC-LAW and struck (adopting the Court of Appeal's construction as binding). (b) The non-redundancy gate
(operative): a surviving clause is lawful only insofar as it adds non-generic procedure (beyond DPR 9.1/9.2/11.3).

**Disposition.** KEEP-RESIDUAL-11.4, narrowed: "the proposer bears the burden and pleads first" survives (DPR 9.1
sets the evidence standard but does not assign *which side* bears it or *who pleads first* on a consolidation
question - non-generic routing); the green-check-is-not-a-holding clause survives, narrowed; the bare
DPR 9.2/11.3 pointers are struck.

**Self-dissent (verbatim).** "The strongest case against my holding is that limb (b) is a distinction without a
workable difference, and the honest disposition is OVERTURN-REMOVE. Once the substantive standard-sentence was
struck under clause-granularity, what remains is genuinely thin, and a future court will struggle to tell a
'non-generic procedural clause' from an artful restatement of one."

## Justice Parsimony (anti-bloat - a rule earns its place by a function, not a pedigree)

**On the objection.** RULE, on a narrower and more sceptical road than the objector; I refuse the objector's
proposed disposition.

**Controlling rule.** *Reference re the Per-Precedent Procedural Rule*. A single conjunctive test, applied at
clause granularity. (a) The non-redundancy gate (the anti-bloat veto, per clause): a clause is lawful only if it
carries procedural content the generic floor does not already encode; a clause restating a generic rule is owned
by that rule and struck (LDD-INV-9; cheapness is not need). **Demonstrated procedural function, not provenance,
earns a clause its place: that a clause came from a ruling (DPR 12.3) is never itself the warrant to enact it.**
(b) The substance-exclusion gate (per clause): a clause restating the originating ruling's substantive standard is
struck and cited.

**Disposition.** KEEP-RESIDUAL-11.4: applying gate (a) clause by clause, the residue is *not* wholly subsumed -
"proposer pleads first" is an ordering/burden-allocation rule DPR 9.1 does not fix; it survives. The Appeals
dissent (remove) is "almost right but not quite, and almost loses."

**Self-dissent (verbatim).** "The honest case against my own holding is that I have done exactly what my seat
exists to veto. Strip the rhetoric and SPEC-LAW-4 says: apply LDD-INV-9 and DPR 11.3 to per-precedent rules,
clause by clause. But LDD-INV-9 already commands one owner per concern. If the generic invariants already do the
work, the new precedent is itself the ceremony it polices."

## Justice Residuum (subsumption - what survives is only what is not entailed elsewhere)

**On the objection.** RULE; the objector is correct on admissibility.

**Controlling rule.** *Reference re the Subsumption Floor for Per-Precedent Procedural Rules*. A clause survives if
and only if it carries procedural content not already entailed by (1) the ground-truth/burden-as-evidence floor
(DPR 9.1), (2) the argue-from-authority floor (DPR 9.2), and (3) the default-consolidation/narrowest-step floor
(DPR 11.3), AND does not restate a substantive SPEC-LAW standard. Subsumption is tested affirmatively by the
proposer (DPR 11.3 burden); a clause repeating a generic rule or a SPEC-LAW standard is surplus and struck.

**Disposition.** KEEP-RESIDUAL-11.4, pruned to its non-subsumed clauses (OTHER). "proposer pleads first" survives
(case-class-specific sequencing the floor leaves open); "on the DPR 9.1 ground-truth standard (assertion is not
evidence)" is struck to the bare "(DPR 9.1)"; "decided as a point of law (DPR 9.2)" struck to a pointer; the
green-check clause survives narrowed.

**Self-dissent (verbatim).** "The strongest case against my holding is the Appeals dissent's, carried to its end:
once the substantive standard-sentence is struck, the residue is so thin that DPR 9.1/9.2/11.3 do subsume all of
it, and my two 'survivors' are illusions. 'Proposer pleads first' is arguably already entailed by DPR 11.3's
default on the non-mover."

## Justice of the Two Registers (constitutional)

**On the objection.** RULE; I name the dismissal trap so no future court mistakes a thin artifact for a small
question.

**Controlling rule.** *Reference re the Two Registers*. A ruling has two essences. Its **substantive essence** (the
standard a future court *applies*) is owned solely by SPEC-LAW and stays there (DPR 12.3, LDD-INV-9). Its
**procedural essence** (how a case is *run*: who pleads, in what order, on what evidentiary standard, in which
register the question is decided, what default fires on failure) is the only thing that may fold into the DPR. The
boundary is constitutional, applied clause by clause, with a four-gate frontier test: (1) procedural not
substantive; (2) non-generic (adds what the floor does not); (3) gated by demonstrated need (DPR 11.3); (4) cites
its owner.

**Disposition.** KEEP-RESIDUAL-11.4, with the heading conformed and the clauses tested: the pleading-order/burden
clause and the green-check-is-not-a-holding clause survive as procedural and non-generic; the restatements of
DPR 9.1/9.2/11.3 are struck to citations.

**Self-dissent (verbatim).** "The honest dissent is that I have built a cathedral on a half-sentence and, in doing
so, committed the exact sin I claim to police. The 'class' I invoke is an artifact of one citation line at
DPR 12.3; three of its four members may themselves not survive their own test, in which case the precedent's first
act is to indict the register that spawned it."
